Abstract

This paper presents a novel bio-inspired kinetic robotic platform that bridges ecological research, kinetic art, and science communication. A modular design workflow is introduced for creating lifelike robotic surrogates, exemplified by a female Houbara bustard. The platform integrates 3D anatomical scanning, parametric CAD modeling, articulated 3D-printed shells, and surface realism through photogrammetric vinyl wraps. Multiple mobility configurations are supported, including rocker-bogie and Mecanum bases. The system embeds RGB and thermal sensing, Jetson-based inference, and a GUI for real-time tracking and synchronized servo control. It has been validated in both ecological trials and public-facing exhibitions, demonstrating its value across biology, engineering, and art.
