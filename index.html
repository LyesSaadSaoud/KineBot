Overview

KineBot is a bio-inspired kinetic robotic platform that merges robotic design, ethology, and kinetic sculpture. Developed as a lifelike replica of the Houbara bustard, the platform is designed to support species-specific interaction studies, educational exhibitions, and public engagement through expressive robotic motion.

Core Contributions

  • Digitally replicable biomimicry pipeline from 3D scanning to articulated printing
  • Surface realism via photogrammetric vinyl wrapping for visual fidelity
  • Rocker-bogie mobility system enabling rough-terrain navigation
  • Thermal + RGB sensing with Jetson-based inference for real-time detection
  • Validated use in controlled ecological trials and kinetic art installations

Authors and Affiliations

The work is a collaboration between Khalifa University (UAE), CNRS (France), and IFHC. The research team combines expertise in robotics, ethology, and interactive systems to develop expressive and behaviorally relevant robotic platforms.
