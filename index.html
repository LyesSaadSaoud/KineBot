KineBot

Bio-Inspired Kinetic Robotics for Artistic, Educational, and Ecological Engagement

Overview

KineBot is a lifelike robotic platform inspired by the Houbara bustard. It serves as a dynamic interface between ecology, kinetic art, and interactive public engagement. This system combines anatomical fidelity, modular design, and real-time embedded intelligence to enable expressive motion, environmental interaction, and multi-context deployment.

Authors

Lyes Saad Saoud, Loïc Lesobre, Enrico Sorato, Saud Al Qaydi, Yves Hingrat, Lakmal Seneviratne, Irfan Hussain

Key Features

  • Digitally replicable pipeline using structured-light 3D scanning and CAD modeling
  • Photorealistic UV-textured vinyl wrapping with matte-finished surface
  • Six-wheeled rocker-bogie locomotion for outdoor terrain
  • Jetson-based visual tracking and object detection (YOLOv11n fine-tuned on custom dataset)
  • Onboard GUI and modular actuation for semi-automated motion control

Applications

KineBot bridges scientific communication and kinetic sculpture. It is designed for:

  • Ethological testing in semi-natural environments
  • Bio-inspired public installations and exhibitions
  • Educational demonstrations in conservation technology
